Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches
  • Backache
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Chills
  • Exhaustion
  • Rash that can appear on the face, palms, soles, and other parts of the body

Prevention

  • Avoid contact with infected animals, particularly those in endemic areas
  • Practice good hygiene such as handwashing after contact with animals or potentially contaminated items
  • Use personal protective equipment when caring for infected patients
  • Avoid eating wild game that could be contaminated

Treatment

  • Currently, there is no specific treatment for monkeypox. Most patients recover without medical treatment.
  • Antiviral drugs and vaccination may be used in some cases to prevent the virus.
  • Supportive treatment focuses on alleviating symptoms such as pain and fever, and keeping the patient hydrated.
