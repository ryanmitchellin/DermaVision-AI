Monkey Pox

Mpox / Monkey Pox is a disease caused by a virus. It usually causes a rash and flu-like symptoms.

Symptoms

The symptoms of mpox include a rash with skin ulcers or blisters, fever, swollen lymph nodes, chills, headache, muscle aches, and fatigue.

What does mpox rash look like?

Usually begins as flat red spots (that can look like pimples or heat rash), which turn into blisters and then form a crust.

How do you get mpox?

Mpox spreads through direct contact with infected sores, scabs, or bodily fluids such as saliva, mucus, blood, or other secretions from an infected person or animal. It can also be transmitted by coming into contact with contaminated materials like clothing, bedding, or towels. Additionally, vertical transmission can occur when a pregnant person passes the virus to their fetus during pregnancy or childbirth.

Chickenpox

Chickenpox is an infection of a virus called varicella-zoster that causes an itchy, blister-like skin rash.

Symptoms

The symptoms include a low-grade fever, tiredness, headache, and a stomachache that reduces appetite. The characteristic itchy skin rash consists of small blisters filled with milky fluid, which eventually break and form scabs. The rash may appear blotchy, and the scabs eventually crust over and fade away.

What does chickenpox rash look like?

The three stages of chickenpox describe the progression of the rash. In Stage 1, a red, bumpy rash appears, lasting for a few days. During Stage 2, the rash develops into fluid-filled blisters, which typically break open after one to two days. Finally, in Stage 3, the blisters scab over, and this stage also lasts a few days.

How do you get chicken pox?

Chickenpox spreads through direct contact with someone who has the virus, inhaling air containing droplets from an infected person's sneeze or cough, and coming into contact with fluids from an infected person's eyes, nose, or mouth.

Measles

Measles (rubeola) is a disease that causes fever and a rash.

Symptoms

The most common measles symptoms include a high fever, tiredness, a barky cough, red or bloodshot eyes, and a runny nose.

What does the measles rash looks like?

The rash starts out as flat red spots on your face and then moves downward to cover your whole body. Then, smaller raised white spots may appear on top of the red rash. The spots might join together as the rash goes down your body.

How do you get measles?

It's highly contagious and spreads through the air when a person with measles talks, coughs or sneezes.

HFMD

HFMD is a disease with blister-like rash that forms on child's hands and feet and painful sores that develop in their mouth.

Symptoms

Hand, foot, and mouth disease symptoms typically appear in two stages. In the initial stage, your child may experience flu-like symptoms, including a mild fever, sore throat, runny nose, stomachache, and loss of appetite.

What does the HFMD rash looks like?

Small red spots or blisters often starting in the mouth then hands and feet.

How do you get HFMD?

Hand, foot, and mouth disease is highly contagious and spreads through airborne droplets when an infected person sneezes or coughs. It can also spread through contact with an infected person's saliva or stool, followed by touching your mouth, eyes, or nose. Direct contact with an infected person's blisters, kissing or hugging someone with the virus, and sharing eating utensils, cups, towels, or clothing also increase the risk. Additionally, touching contaminated toys, surfaces, doorknobs, or other items and then touching your eyes, nose, or mouth can lead to transmission.

Cowpox

Cowpox is a rare zoonotic infection caused by a DNA orthopoxvirus that is similar to the vaccinia and smallpox viruses.

Symptoms

Symptoms include fatigue, flu, headache, malaise, and regional lymphadenopathy.

What does the cowpox rash looks like?

The cowpox rash typically begins as small, red spots that develop into raised, fluid-filled blisters or pustules. These lesions usually appear on the hands, arms, or face, but they can spread to other parts of the body. Over time, the blisters form scabs that eventually fall off as the skin heals. The rash may be accompanied by mild symptoms such as fever, fatigue, and swollen lymph nodes. The lesions are usually less severe than those of smallpox and may not be as widespread.

How do you get cowpox?

Cowpox primarily spreads through direct contact with infected animals, particularly cows, although other animals like rodents and cats can also carry the virus. Humans can contract cowpox by handling infected animals or their contaminated materials, such as fur, skin, or saliva. The virus can enter through broken skin or mucous membranes. Additionally, human-to-human transmission is possible through contact with infected lesions or bodily fluids, although this is less common. The risk of transmission is typically higher in settings where people have close contact with animals that may carry the virus.
